Know before you Grow!

11 varieties classified with precision!

abrus precatorius

Abrus precatorius, also known as the rosary pea, is a highly toxic plant found in Southern and Eastern India. Its consumption, can be lethal to humans due to its neurotoxic activity.

madagascar periwinkle

Madagascar periwinkle, present throughout India, has a low poison level. However, consuming this cytotoxic plant can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach pain.

castor oil plant

Castor oil plant, found throughout India, is a highly poisonous plant with both cytotoxic and Ricin toxin activities. Eating its seeds can lead to severe abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea

Dieffenbachia

Dieffenbachia, found throughout India, is an irritant plant that, when chewed, can cause swelling and burning of the mouth and throat breathing difficulty and corneal damage.

Foxglove

Foxglove, found in the Himalayan region and Western Ghats, contains cardiac glycosides that can cause irregular heartbeat and death if leaves or seeds are ingested.

Lilies

Lilies, present throughout India, are nephrotoxic plants that can cause kidney damage in cats if ingested.

Oleander

A highly toxic plant found throughout India. Ingesting any part of the plant can cause nausea, vomiting, and irregular heartbeat due to its cardiac glycosides properties.

Pothos

A low-poison plant found throughout India, but chewing the leaves can cause swelling and burning of the mouth and throat due to its irritant properties.

Rhubarb

A low-poison plant found throughout India, but eating large amounts of leaves can cause kidney damage due to its oxalates properties.

lily of the valley

Abrus precatorius, also known as the rosary pea, is a highly toxic plant found in Southern and Eastern India. Its consumption, even a single seed, can be lethal to humans due to its neurotoxic activity

Wisteria

A highly toxic plant found in the Himalayan region and Western Ghats. Ingesting the seeds or pods can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and confusion due to its neurotoxic properties.
